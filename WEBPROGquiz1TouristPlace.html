Tourist Places to Visit

Monas

Monas, short for Monumen Nasional, is an iconic landmark and national monument located in the heart of Jakarta. It stands as a symbol of Indonesia's struggle for independence and is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the city. The monument consists of a towering obelisk topped with a golden flame, which represents the nation's independence. Visitors can take an elevator to the top of the monument for panoramic views of Jakarta. The surrounding Merdeka Square is a popular gathering place for locals and tourists alike.

Kota Tua

Kota Tua, also known as Jakarta Old Town or Batavia, is a historic district in Jakarta that dates back to the Dutch colonial period. It features well-preserved colonial-era buildings, cobblestone streets, and charming town squares. Visitors can explore museums, such as the Jakarta History Museum (Fatahillah Museum), Bank Indonesia Museum, and Puppet Museum, to learn about the city's history. Kota Tua is also home to quaint cafes, street vendors, and street performers, making it a popular destination for both history enthusiasts and tourists seeking a glimpse of Jakarta's past.

Dufan

Dufan, or Dunia Fantasi in Indonesian, is Jakarta's largest amusement park and a popular destination for families and thrill seekers. It is located in Ancol Dreamland and includes a range of rides and attractions such as roller coasters, water rides, a Ferris wheel, and entertainment acts. Dufan is separated into themed zones for tourists of all ages, such as Fantasy World, Adventure Land, and Science Land.
