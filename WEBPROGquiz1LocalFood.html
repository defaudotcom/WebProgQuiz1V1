Local food

Nasi Goreng

Nasi Goreng, which translates to "fried rice" in English, is a popular and iconic Indonesian meal. In Jakarta, you may get a variety of Nasi Goreng dishes, each with its own distinct flavour. It is commonly made up of stir-fried rice with shallots, garlic, prawns, chicken and a variety of spices and seasonings such as kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), tamarind and chilli. It's frequently topped with a fried egg and served with prawn crackers (krupuk) and cucumber slices. Nasi Goreng is a tasty, satisfying, and popular meal found in Jakarta's street food vendors, restaurants, and food courts.

Soto Betawi

Soto Betawi is a typical Jakartan soup with a rich, creamy broth. It's cooked with beef (typically a combination of flesh, tripe, and offal), coconut milk, and an aromatic spice blend that includes lemongrass, galangal, and candlenut. The soup is often served with rice and garnished with fried shallots, lime leaves, and, for added heat, sambal (chilli paste). Soto Betawi is a soothing and hearty dish that reflects the unique flavours of Jakarta and is enjoyed by both locals and visitors.

Kerak Telor

Kerak Telor is a traditional Betawi snack that you should have if you visit Jakarta. It's a savoury glutinous rice cake prepared with duck or chicken eggs. Before cooking on a charcoal barbecue, the ingredients are combined with spices, shredded coconut, and fried shallots. Kerak Telor is distinguished by its somewhat crispy, crusty texture on the outside, while the inside stays soft and tasty. It's typically served on a banana leaf and is popular at festivals or as street food in Jakarta. Kerak Telor is a wonderful and unusual Jakarta snack due to its combination of textures and flavours.
